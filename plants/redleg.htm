Bothriochloa macra
Family: POACEAE
This grass has finger-like inflorescences (flower-bearing systems.)
Bothriochloa macra or Red-leg Grass
Native. Widespread in overgrazed pastures.
From the Greek bothrion meaning little pit and chloe meaning grass, referring to the joints and pedicels having a distinct discoloured groove (pedicel being the stalk of a flower).
